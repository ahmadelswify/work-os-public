# work-os for Any Professional

A flexible guide for using work-os in any professional role.

## Why work-os Works for Everyone

No matter your role, you're likely dealing with:
- Multiple projects and priorities
- Meetings and stakeholder communication
- Career development and growth
- The need to demonstrate impact

work-os adapts to your specific work, not the other way around.

### The Brain Dump Habit
Throughout your day, capture everything in BACKLOG.md:
```
- Great conversation with stakeholder X, they want feature Y
- Noticed process inefficiency in workflow Z
- Need to follow up on last week's proposal
- Idea: create dashboard for team metrics
- Research needed: best practices for [topic]
```

**The rule:** Capture first, organize later.

## Adapting to Your Role

### Just Tell Claude About Your Work

No manual setup required. Just describe what you do:

```
You: "I'm a [your role]. My main work involves [category 1], [category 2],
     and [category 3]. Urgent things for me are [what's urgent].
     Right now I'm focused on [current priorities]."

Claude: "I'll set up your workspace based on your work..."
```

Claude will:
1. Create `Active/` folders that match your actual work categories
2. Set up your GOALS.md with your priorities
3. Learn what "urgent" means for YOUR role
4. Adapt the system to how YOU work

**No forms. No manual folder creation. Just conversation.**

## Universal Tips

1. **Match structure to your reality** - Don't force yourself into someone else's system
2. **Brain dump constantly** - Capture ideas and tasks when they occur
3. **Process daily** - Keep your backlog manageable
4. **Review weekly** - Reflect and adjust
5. **Track wins** - Log accomplishments for reviews and career growth
6. **Link to goals** - Every task should support something meaningful
